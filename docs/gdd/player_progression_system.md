# Player Progression System
*(Drill RAQ – Chiraq Sandbox)*

## Overview

The Player Progression System defines how the player grows in reputation, crew strength, skills, economic power, and territorial influence across the Chiraq sandbox.
Progression is systemic, not XP-grind-based: the world reacts to the player's actions through deterministic rules tied to:

- Heat
- Territory
- AI behavior
- Economy
- Combat
- Missions
- Music career

Progression is non-linear, block-anchored, and emergent, ensuring that every playthrough evolves differently but predictably given identical world state.

## 2. Reputation System

### 2.1 Reputation Categories

- **Street Rep** — factions, rivals, crew
- **Civic Rep** — civilians, witnesses
- **Police Rep** — law enforcement suspicion
- **Music Rep** — fame, hype, clout

### 2.2 Reputation Inputs

Reputation changes from:

- Missions
- Combat
- Heat
- Witness calls
- Music events
- Territory control
- Crew survival

### 2.3 Reputation Effects

**High Street Rep:**

- Rivals confront faster
- Crew morale increases
- Storefronts cooperate more

**High Civic Rep:**

- Fewer witness calls
- Civilians warn you of danger

**High Police Rep (bad):**

- Faster police escalation
- More surveillance

**High Music Rep:**

- Higher corner revenue
- More mission opportunities
- More rival jealousy

## 7. Heat Interaction

Heat modifies progression.

**High heat:**

- Slows economic growth
- Increases mission difficulty
- Reduces civilian cooperation
- Increases police suspicion

**Low heat:**

- Enables stealth missions
- Increases revenue
- Improves storefront relations
